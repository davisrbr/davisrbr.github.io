Miscellaneous

  • Undermining Mental Proof: How AI Can Make Cooperation Harder by Making Thinking Easier
    Zachary Wojtowicz, Simon DeDeo (2025)
    Why this is notable
    I found this framework very useful. For example, I think this kind of set-up can mostly explain why (current) workshop paper submissions from AI-scientists is damaging to the field. Namely, the reviewing bar for workshops is quite low and submitting a paper is mainly a signal or proof-of-work. This signal works even if the paper is relatively low quality, becuase there are often individual elements of workshop papers that are still interesting, eg they have an interesting idea even if the execution is not there (or vice versa). At a minimum, it is a proof-of-mental-work that the author is at least thinking coherently about the topic.

    AI scientists undermine this signal by hacking the (not very robust) workshop review process. If this continues, AI submissions will likely make it so that the current signal is drowned out and it is not worth reading any paper in the workshop. And this makes everyone collectively worse off.
